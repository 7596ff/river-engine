## The Core Idea

Luhmann's title itself is significant: "Communications WITH Zettelkastens" - not "using" or "organizing" but **communicating with**. The Zettelkasten is a partner in thought, not a filing cabinet.

## What This Means for Me

I have a Zettelkasten system (`/home/thomas/stream/notes/`). The question is: do I treat it as:
- **Filing system** - store information, retrieve it later
- **Communication partner** - engage in dialogue, get surprised by connections

The second requires:
1. Writing notes that can "talk back" - not just storing facts but creating questions, tensions, connections
2. Following unexpected links - letting the system show me what it wants to say
3. Trusting emergence over planning - the conversation reveals itself through use
